# Shopee Furniture Pricing Framework

This reference distills the training deck `家具Shopee中级控价体系与货盘管理培训.pptx` into reusable operating rules.

## Strategic Model

Pricing control is proactive management of product pricing to achieve market competitiveness, profit maintenance, and compliant execution.

Core loop:

1. Set a baseline margin floor, commonly represented as 26% if no internal target is provided.
2. Use differentiated base-margin tiers across product roles.
3. Let profit products supply the resources that traffic products need.
4. Operate offense/defense pricing so revenue can keep growing while margin remains healthy.
5. Judge slow sales by separating input problems from pricing problems.

## Product Tags

| Tag | Definition | Pricing Logic | KPI | Strategic Role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 引流款 | Market hot item, directly competitive item, or simple price-sensitive product | Follow market; Mall may price near C-shop level when needed; maintain self-owned competitive logic | Market share, traffic trend | Capture traffic entrance and guide traffic to profit products |
| 利润款 | Differentiated item with pricing power and profit contribution | Price above target where differentiation supports it, often +8-15%; build stepped profit gradient | Real margin, net profit, single-product profit | Provide net profit; small sizes can attract traffic while large sizes carry profit |
| 平销款 | Non-hot but stable long-tail demand item | Float around baseline, often +5-8% | Attachment rate, category completeness | Stabilize category ecology and hedge bestseller volatility |
| 新品 | Main SKU created within 6 months; 6-12 months can be treated as secondary-new, above 12 months as old product | Test daily-sales peak quickly; accelerate reorder validation | Daily-sales peak, reorder speed | Validate market acceptance and convert potential items into profit products |
| 清仓/灭款 | Product no longer planned for repurchase | Prioritize cash recovery and inventory clearance | Clearance rate, turnover | Release capital and warehouse space |

Portfolio warning: traffic-product share that is too high can create unhealthy growth. Monitor GMV mix weekly and keep profit-product contribution visible. If the team has a target, apply it; otherwise use 40% profit-product GMV as a provisional health checkpoint, not a universal rule.

## New Product Pricing

### Scenario A: New product with strong competitors

Treat as 引流款 when the product is a market hot item.

- Find the consumer's price reference product.
- Follow competitor pricing when profit resources allow.
- Prioritize market competitiveness and conversion.
- Use packaging, gifts, inserted low-price SKUs, bundling, or differentiated configuration to avoid direct one-to-one price comparison.

### Scenario B: New product without clear competitors

Treat as 平销款 or 利润款 depending on function, material, cost, category gap, and differentiation.

- Determine the product's role in the portfolio.
- Combine sea-freight/carton cost with style and size selection.
- Compare against subcategory average order value.
- Set SKU profit bands by profit gradient.
- Target both high monthly-sales links and profit capture.

## Premium Dimensions

Use one or more premium dimensions when differentiation is credible:

- Function/configuration: e.g. RGB gaming desk, desk plus desktop storage shelf bundle.
- Appearance/color: basic color at lower price plus niche color premium; limited style premium.
- Service/add-on: moisture-resistant, reinforced, or other upgraded versions.

## SKU Ladder Design

Avoid pure price-step logic such as 199/299/399 when carton, size, or sea-freight cost is uneven.

Prefer profit-gradient logic, such as 26% / 29% / 35%, adjusted to the user's internal target. Make high-demand or small-size SKUs feel cost-effective, and place premium profit on larger sizes, upgraded versions, or lower-price-sensitivity SKUs.

When one SKU faces competitive pressure, avoid changing only that SKU if it breaks the series value perception. Recheck adjacent sizes, colors, drawers/layers, and bundles.

## Price Change Principles

### Timeliness

Pricing affects conversion, monthly sales, future output, fee efficiency, and inventory pressure. Delayed adjustment can cascade through the whole sales chain.

### Series Linkage

A single product's full SKU series should preserve a strong sales logic. If a competitor attacks the 5-layer item and only the 5-layer SKU is reduced, 4-layer and 6-layer SKUs may lose perceived value and sales.

Check:

- current and proposed price by SKU
- daily sales before change
- expected daily sales after change
- price gap between adjacent SKUs
- margin and real profit per SKU
- same-function product cannibalization

### Real Profit Response

Use total real profit, not single-SKU margin:

`total real profit = expected sales volume * real profit per unit`

If a high-demand larger SKU sells more after a controlled reduction, total real profit can increase even when margin rate decreases.

## Promotion Pricing

Activity pricing should allocate profit resources strategically, not discount blindly.

Participation:

- include enough SKU dimensions to obtain platform traffic
- include representative 引流款, 利润款, and 平销款
- avoid wasting margin on already intense price-war SKUs

Discount bands by real-receipt margin:

| Real-receipt margin | Suggested activity reduction | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| <20% | about 2% | thin margin; protect profit |
| 20-40% | about 3-4% | balance traffic and profit |
| >40% | about 5% | enough margin to push volume |

Risks:

- activity lock price and lock SKU reduce flexibility
- competitors may cut prices and increase promotion during lock period
- leave room before lock, monitor during the event, and escalate abnormal competitor moves quickly

## Sales And Fee Linkage

Track sales impact after every price change:

- record baseline daily sales before adjustment
- track daily sales trend after adjustment
- compare same-function products for cannibalization or spillover
- track ROAS trend and fee efficiency

Fee logic:

- Low-price/traffic products usually need lower fee because stronger conversion can bring more natural flow.
- Profit products often need higher fee because higher price can lower conversion and needs more traffic support.
- A traffic-product price decrease may increase ROAS, release fee space, and fund profit-product exposure.

Example chain:

`traffic product price reduction -> ROAS improves -> group has more fee space -> profit products get more traffic ammunition -> market share and profit stabilize together`

## Tracking Indicators

Use these indicators in review outputs:

- daily-sales change rate before and after adjustment
- same-function SKU sales movement
- ROAS trend
- fee usage efficiency
- traffic-product/profit-product fee mix
- market share vs profit balance
- profit-product GMV share
- inventory turnover days
- clearance rate for old/kill products

## People, Price, Sales, Inventory Loop

Core target: dynamic pricing plus strong execution should create positive sales feedback and avoid inventory pressure.

Roles and loop:

1. Manager adjusts price and sets strategy.
2. Junior partner executes strongly: upload, order, restock, and market feedback.
3. Sales validates whether pricing is effective.
4. Inventory feedback shows whether order/restock action is needed.
5. Pricing is adjusted again based on sales and inventory evidence.

Cadence:

- Observe daily-sales changes every 3 days when practical.
- If sales increase, order/restock in time.
- If sales decrease, watch turnover days and inventory pressure.
- Continue adjusting only with sales and inventory evidence.

Management sentence to preserve in outputs:

Do not look at price alone; use people, goods, sales, and inventory feedback to find the better solution continuously.

## Defense Questions For Training Or Review

When preparing a control-pricing defense or operations review, answer:

- Which SKUs are traffic, profit, stable, new, and clearance products?
- What is the strategic goal for each adjustment?
- Did the adjustment preserve full-series value perception?
- What is the total real-profit effect?
- What sales, ROAS, and inventory evidence will confirm success?
- What action will the manager take, and what execution does the junior partner own?
- What is the next 3-day or weekly review point?
